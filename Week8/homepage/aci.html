It's strange how certain moments stick with you. I was only 11 when TAM 3054 crashed, and I watched it unfold live on TV. That event, tangled with fear and fascination, sparked my enduring interest in Air Crash Investigations (ACI).

There's something haunting about the idea of being on a doomed flight – the rush of emotions, the finality. It's a chilling thought, yet it drives my curiosity about how these tragedies are meticulously dissected and understood.

The detective work in ACI grips me: investigators sifting through wreckage, decoding black box data, reconstructing events, and all of this after some disheartening tragedy. We learn, we improve, we prevent.

The unsolved mysteries, the rare mid-air collisions, the vastness of our skies and the delicacy of our travels within them. Every investigation can shape the future of aviation.

This isn't just a hobby; it's a homage to those lost in these tragedies and an opportunity to learn.
